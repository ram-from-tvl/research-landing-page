Unlocking efficient solar energy deployment with data-efficient forecasting.

About the Project

Traditional solar forecasting models demand years of site-specific historical irradiance data, a luxury often unavailable for newer photovoltaic (PV) farms. With solar energy's inherent intermittency, developing accurate and data-efficient forecasting systems is crucial for robust grid management and to accelerate the global proliferation of solar power, directly supporting the United Nations' net-zero goals.

In this groundbreaking work, we introduce SPIRIT, a novel framework that leverages state-of-the-art foundation models for solar irradiance forecasting. This innovative approach makes accurate prediction applicable even to the most recent solar installations, eliminating the reliance on extensive historical data.

Our findings demonstrate that SPIRIT significantly outperforms existing state-of-the-art models in zero-shot transfer learning scenarios, achieving performance improvements of up to 70%. This enables effective forecasting at new locations without any prior site-specific data. Furthermore, performance sees additional enhancements through fine-tuning as more location-specific data becomes available over time. These results are backed by rigorous statistical significance, validating the robustness of our method.

By dramatically reducing the forecasting setup timeline, SPIRIT has the potential to accelerate solar farm deployment across potential global sites—many of which currently lack the necessary historical data. This innovation is a pivotal step towards democratizing access to clean energy and fostering broader participation in the renewable energy transition.
